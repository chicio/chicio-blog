## <ParagraphTitleWithIcon icon={<FaLightbulb className="text-shadow-lg" />}>Trivia & Fun Facts</ParagraphTitleWithIcon>

- Before its official reveal, the Wii was codenamed "Revolution." Nintendo chose the final name "Wii" because the two "i"s looked like two people standing side by side, symbolizing its focus on multiplayer and social play
- Wii Sports is one of the best-selling games of all time (over 82 million copies), but in most regions, it wasn't sold separately—it was a pack-in title. It was so effective at demonstrating motion controls that it was used in retirement homes and hospitals for physical therapy.
- Under the hood, the Wii was essentially a heavily "overclocked" GameCube. This made it natively backward compatible with GameCube games and controllers (on the original models), making it a 2-in-1 console for many fans.
